Development of ultra-high field (UHF) strength (7 Tesla) MRI scanners has increased the possibilities, compared to conventional MRI scanners, to obtain structural and blood oxygenation level-dependent (BOLD) functional MRI (fMRI) data with an unprecedented spatial resolution, without compromising data quality or increasing scanning time. In addition, novel post-processing strategies that enable analyses of complex and convoluted structures become more and more available. Part of my research is dedicated to optimization and validation of UHF=MRI techniques. Several of my on-going projects are highlighted below

— Multi-parametric UHF MRI protocol (in collaboration with dr. Kamil Uludag and dr. Dimo Ivanov)

I will setup an optimized multi-parametric UHF MRI protocol using acquisition schemes that capture fine-scale properties of the hippocampal formation. Here, prime focus will be on the implementation and optimization of existing vascular imaging methods. These will include time-of-flight, susceptibility-weighted and arterial spin labeling imaging. High spatial resolution will be crucial for the proposed experiments, as the structures of interest are small. Image post-processing methods will be optimized to (a) preserve the spatial specificity of the resulting artery, vein and cerebral blood flow (CBF) maps, (b) allow implementation in an in-house developed hippocampal formation coordinate system4 and (c) be combined with other parametric maps.

— Probing subfield integrity

In healthy humans (N=20), I will obtain and analyze the multi-parametric UHF MRI data combined with high resolution resting-state fMRI (rs-fMRI) and behavioral data. Recent work has shown that high resolution rs-fMRI imaging allows for assessing connectivity between hippocampal subfields7. Here, I will validate these findings by disentangling contributions from neuronal activity and other physiological effects such as macroscopic vasculature. In addition, behavioral measures to probe distinct memory subprocesses, such as mismatch detection, pattern separation, and pattern completion, will be employed to correlate with hippocampal subfield integrity based on the UHF MRI data.
